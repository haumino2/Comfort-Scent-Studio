The logic chain
01

User insight

Shoppers don't choose fabric care by chemistry. They choose how clothes make them feel — and scent is the social signal others notice first.

02

Identity category

Fresh, gentle, floral, confident — each maps to a personal identity shoppers already carry. Comfort sits at the intersection of laundry ritual and self-expression.

03

Category pressure

The aisle demands a fast decision. Too many variants, similar packaging, and a 30–60 second window before habit or confusion wins.

04

Platform response

Scent Studio collapses education, fit, and trial into one QR moment — live shelf, real signals, physical scent proof.

Why scent is the connective tissue

Fabric care is functional on the label but emotional in the basket. Scent is how laundry becomes a personal identity category — and a social one. Clothes carry fragrance into every room, meeting, and family moment. Comfort doesn't just clean; it finishes the story the shopper tells about themselves.

◈

Personal identity

"I want to feel fresh and put-together" or "I want home to smell welcoming" — scent preference reveals persona faster than any SKU comparison.

◎

Social signal

Fragrance on fabric is noticed by others before it's articulated. Shoppers are buying confidence, warmth, or elegance — not just rinse-cycle performance.

◇

In-store moment

At the bay, there is no time for a quiz. The category needs a shortcut: read the shopper, surface the right variant, and let them decide in seconds.

Signals that make advice feel intelligent

Short inputs alone are powerful. Scent Studio layers contextual facts — responsibly, claim-safe — so recommendations feel aware of the shopper's world, not generic.

Environment API

Live humidity and temperature from Open-Meteo contextualise drying conditions. On humid days, indoor-drying friendly variants rise — without overclaiming product performance.

Example: "Humid weather detected. Indoor-drying friendly options are slightly rising."

Style / outfit scan

Optional camera scan reads wardrobe aesthetic — minimal, soft, structured — as an inspiration signal only. It nudges fit; it does not replace shopper inputs.

Example: "Your outfit leans minimal and soft. Pure Gentle Care rises as an inspiration match."

Persona resolution

Five scent personas — Everyday Refresher, Gentle Homemaker, Fresh Routine Optimizer, and more — bridge laundry behaviour to social identity language the brand can own.

Persona emerges step by step: "You may be The Fresh Routine Optimizer."

From digital fit to physical scent

The gap in fabric care is sensory — shoppers cannot smell the bottle through packaging. Scent Studio closes that gap by connecting the recommended Comfort profile to a physical diffuser at the stand.

Think of it as a Comfort scent capsule experience: the matched variant triggers a calibrated fragrance release on a connected device — similar to how premium beauty uses essential-oil stations for trial.

Try the interactive demo → select a product match and release scent to see how the bay moment works.

  • Best-match SKU maps to a scent profile on the in-store diffuser
  • Shopper smells the recommendation before they buy — reducing regret, increasing conversion
  • Future path: Comfort Scent Diffuser as a loyalty and home-extension touchpoint
  • Claim-safe copy throughout — inspiration signals, not medical or performance claims
